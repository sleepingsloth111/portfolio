Project: Responsive Redesign

Introduction

Looking for a website to redesign, I thought about my family's favorite Vietnamese restaurant in my hometown, Pho Empire. I looked up the site and noticed some issues on their menu page. From there, I took note of key problems, designed low-fidelity wireframes, made high-fidelity prototypes, and implemented the redesigned website.

Part 1: Addressing Key Issues

I analyzed the webpage and took note of the key problems in the design. I chose to redesign this page because of the large amount of disorder in the alignment of elements and the lack of focus on the menu category images.

Picking a Webpage

Using usability, learnability, efficiency, and memorability as my criterias, I listed the parts of the page that failed to fulfill them.

  • Hard to efficiently and easily identify elements with the distracting and detailed background image.
  • Difficult to efficiently identify elements visually with transparent boxes as element backgrounds.
  • Unable to quickly click on menu category item as text goes over transparent box backgrounds.
  • Bottom bar is not webresponsive and shifts locations for screens smaller than desktop.
  • Lack of hierarchy from menu title and descriptions being on the left side disrupting memorability and efficiency in reading.
  • Disorder from varying alignment and sizes for menu category elements which causes delay in usability and efficiency.
  • Difficult to memorize menu category layout as borderless image is laid on background image.
  • The entire page fits for any screen size so mobile users will have a hard time clicking tiny buttons and viewing images

Accessibility

The Web Accessibility Evaluation Tool (WAVE) report mainly stated of missing alt text for buttons and images, which I think is unaccessible. If a user's vision is impaired, they should be able to accurately hear the screenreader state the alt tag of the image for each menu section. Additionally, the button and text of “Online Order” had low contrast which I was surprised about since it seems quite distinct to my eyes.

Part 2: Visual Redesign

Though eager to start working on a redesigned site, I knew there were many steps before jumping onto VS Code. Addressing the problems from Part 1, I designed low-fidelity wireframes, a visual design style guide, and high-fidelity prototypes.

Low Fidelity Wireframing

After thinking of designs, I looked at Chile's and Olive Garden's menu page to see how large businesses design responsive webpages for inspiration. Then, using Balsamiq, I created wireframes for 3 sizes: monitor, tablet, and mobile. I also decided to annotate the wireframes for how each component resolves the issues I found.

Visual Design Style Guide

Before moving onto making my high-fidelity prototypes, I made a design style guide as part of the process. I decided to retain the color scheme of the original website and also followed the design of Bootstrap components.

High Fidelity Prototyping

Feeling confident with my design style guide, I moved forward with creating high-fidelity prototypes on Figma. I decided to also annotate the prototype for key elements with their class and css style. In my annotations, I often focused on how this relates to making the page responsive.
